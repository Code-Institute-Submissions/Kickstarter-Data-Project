Welcome to Kickstarted!

Kickstarted is a data dashboard that uses project submission data from the popular fund raising site Kickstarter and displays it in an easy to digest format.

For those who don't know, Kickstarter is an American public-benefit corporation based in Brooklyn, New York, that maintains a global crowdfunding platform focused on creativity and merchandising. The company's stated mission is to "help bring creative projects to life".

On Kickstarter, you only get to see the life of a single project at a time. So with Kickstarted, I wanted to be able to see the life of the site itself with information on popular submission times, success and failure rates as well as some other charts to see can we notice any trends.

To interact with the charts and data, simply click on different bars, slices and sections. This will effectively filter the data set by your selection. To reverse this, simply click on Reset Charts. For more information on what each chart represents, click the info icon!

Project Submission Timeline : September 2016 - January 2018

Current the site is using data from 60,000 + projects that have been submitted between September 2016 and January 2018. This timeline gives us some idea of when during the year are project managers pushing their product or idea to the world!

It is worth noting that there are hundreds of thousands of projects on Kickstarter and so this representation is not indicitive of the site's performance overall.

Project Funding Results

Once a project is accepted and goes live on Kickstarter, it can end in a number of ways such as Successful, Failed, Cancelled or Suspended.

Successful projects have reached or exceeded their funding goal within their given time limit and will receive the pledged amount, minus Kickstarter deductions, for the project that was advertised.

Failed projects are those which didn't achieve their funding goal amount within the time frame. These are removed and all monies are returned to that projects backers.

Cancelled projects are a group where very real issues can cause those even with the best of intentions to realise long term, their idea was not sustainable and thus backed out. There are other issues that cause cancellations such as scams. A famous one being the story of Mythic: The Story of Gods and Men. More on this and other well known cancelled projects can be found here.

Suspended projects.Last but not least there are those that are suspended by Kickstarter themsevles. This happens when a project is found to be violating the terms put in place by Kickstarter. For a list of these terms, click here.

Submission Categories

On Kickstarter, there are a number of different categories you can browse to view projects that may interest you. For this project, we use a range of different categories from Games and Technology to Theater and Journalism.

This row chart shows us the amount of submissions per category between September of 2016 and January of 2018. As you interact with the charts, we can see which categories were most and least popular.

For example, if we click on Great Britain we can see that the top 3 categories were Games, Film and Video, and Publishing.
